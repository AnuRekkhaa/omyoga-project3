IMPORTANT AASANAS

Marjaryasana-Bitilasana

Marjaryasana-Bitilasana, or Cat-Cow Pose, is a gentle flow between two positions that warms the spine and relieves tension in the back. This dynamic pose enhances flexibility and promotes mindful breathing.

Position:Arch your back, lift your tailbone and head toward the ceiling, allowing your belly to drop toward the mat.

Utkatasana

Utkatasana or Chair Pose, is a powerful standing asana that builds strength and stability while promoting focus and determination. This pose mimics the action of sitting in an imaginary chair, making it both challenging and invigorating.

Position:Inhale as you bend your knees, pushing your hips back as if sitting in a chair and extend your arms overhead, keeping them parallel or palms together.

Viparita Karani

Viparita Karani, or Legs Up the Wall Pose, is a restorative yoga asana that promotes relaxation and rejuvenation. This gentle inversion encourages the body to unwind while providing numerous physical and mental benefits.

Position:Your back should be on the floor, and your buttocks should be up towards the ceiling, forming an L shape with your body.

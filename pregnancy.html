PRENATAL PEACE

Pregnancy Yoga is a gentle and supportive practice designed specifically for expectant mothers. This class focuses on safe, modified poses that promote physical strength, flexibility, and relaxation during pregnancy. Participants will learn breathing techniques to enhance connection with their baby and reduce stress, along with movements that alleviate common discomforts like back pain and fatigue.

BENEFITS
1.Increases Body Awareness: Fosters a deeper connection with the body and baby, promoting mindfulness throughout pregnancy.
2.Prepares for Labor: Teaches breathing techniques and positions that can be beneficial during labor and delivery.
3.Supports Recovery: Aids in postpartum recovery by promoting physical and mental well-being.
4.Enhances Strength: Strengthens core and pelvic floor muscles, supporting the growing belly and reducing back pain.

Yoga for Every Trimester

1.Gentle Asanas: Tailored poses that strengthen the body while accommodating the changes of pregnancy.
2.Breathing Exercises: Techniques to improve lung capacity and promote relaxation.
3.Mindfulness Practices: Meditation and visualization to enhance emotional well-being.
4.Community Support: A nurturing environment where mothers-to-be can connect and share experiences.

Tips:
1.Listen to Your Body: Pay attention to how you feel and modify poses as needed. It’s okay to skip certain movements if they don’t feel right.
2.Focus on Breath: Use deep, calming breaths to enhance relaxation and connect with your baby throughout the practice.
3.Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water before and after your yoga session to stay hydrated.

IMPORTANT AASANAS

Janu sirsasana

1st Trimester

Place the sole of left foot against inner right thigh.Inhale as you reach arms overhead.Exhale to fold forward slightly, lengthening the front of your torso.Place your hands on your body or the floor.

Baddha Konasana

2nd Trimester

Sit on the cushion block or folded blanket, allowing your pelvis to tilt forward.Press the soles of your feet.Bring feet in toward hips to deepen the stretch.Root your lower body as you lengthen your spine.

Malasana

3rd Trimester

Stand with feet slightly wider than hips and toes turned out at an angle.Slowly bend knees and lower your hips.Lift your heels or place them on the floor.Press palms at the center of your chest.Press elbows into knees.

LITTLE YOGIS

Kids yoga is a fun and engaging practice designed to promote physical, mental, and emotional well-being in children. Through playful movements, breathing exercises, and imaginative themes, kids yoga encourages creativity, mindfulness, and body awareness.

BENEFITS
1.Encourages Social Skills: Fosters teamwork and communication through group activities and partner poses.
2.Enhances Flexibility: Gentle stretches in the morning promote flexibility and reduce stiffness after a night's rest.
3.Builds Confidence: Boosts self-esteem as kids learn new poses and overcome challenges in a supportive environment.
4.Enhances Focus: Encourages concentration and mindfulness, helping children stay present in the moment.

Your Evening Yoga Routine

1.Fun Poses: Incorporate animal poses, like "Downward Dog" and "Tree Pose," to make practice enjoyable and relatable.
2.Creative Themes: Use stories and themes (e.g., journeys, adventures) to engage kids' imaginations.
3.Breathing Exercises: Introduce simple breathing techniques to promote relaxation and mindfulness..

Tips:
1.Make It Playful: Use games and interactive activities to keep kids engaged and excited about yoga.
2.Encourage Creativity: Allow kids to express themselves and create their own poses or stories.
3.Keep It Light: Focus on fun and exploration rather than perfection in poses.

IMPORTANT AASANAS

Horse Riding Pose

Bend forward and place both hands by side of both feet.Stretch the right leg back as much as possible.Bring the right knee on the floor. Bend the left leg at the knee.Inhale raise the head backward with the back arched and gaze in between the eyebrows.

Cat pose

Kneel on the floor and lean forward putting both your palms on the floor pointing forward.The legs can be slightly apart and palms should be at shoulder length. Let your trunk be parallel to the ground, the thighs should be straight.

Rabbit pose

Sit in kneeling posture.Spread both the knees wide apart, keep the big toes touching.Keep the palms between the knees. Exhale and slowly stretch them to full length.Bend forward and place the chin on the ground.Keep the arms parallel.

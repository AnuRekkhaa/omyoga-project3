AWAKEN YOUR SPIRIT

Morning yoga is a refreshing practice that sets a positive tone for the day ahead. Engaging in yoga in the morning helps awaken the body, invigorate the mind, and cultivate a sense of mindfulness and intention.

BENEFITS
1.Increases Energy: Morning yoga stimulates circulation and energizes the body, preparing you for the day.
2.Enhances Flexibility: Gentle stretches in the morning promote flexibility and reduce stiffness after a night's rest.
3.Improves Focus: Starting the day with yoga helps clear the mind, improving concentration and mental clarity.
4.Reduces Stress: Establishing a morning routine with yoga can reduce anxiety and promote a sense of calm.

Your Morning Yoga Guide

1.Gentle Warm-ups: Begin with simple stretches to awaken the body.
2.Sun Salutations: Flow through a series of sun salutations to energize and warm up the muscles.
3.Standing Poses: Incorporate standing poses to build strength and stability.
4.Balance Poses: Include balancing asanas to improve focus and coordination.
5.Closing Poses: End with calming poses or seated meditation to center yourself.

Tips:
1.Create a Calm Space: Find a peaceful spot to practice, free from distractions.
2.Set Intentions: Take a moment to set intentions for the day during your practice.
3.Listen to Your Body: Be gentle and mindful, adapting poses to your comfort level.

IMPORTANT AASANAS

Happy Baby Pose

Lie on your back on your mat.Bend your knees and bring them to your stomach, gripping the outsides of your feet. Flex your heels and ankles. Breathe here, focusing on keeping your ankles directly above your knees while pushing against your hands with your feet.

Garland Pose

Squat down with your feet as close together as you can get them, toes pointed out.Let your torso fall between your thighs, pressing your elbows against your knees. Keep your tailbone pressed toward the ground and your chest up, use your knees to help you.

Easy Chair Pose

Stand with your feet together and inhale, extending your arms directly overhead.Exhale, begin to sit back into your hips, and bend your knees. Stop when your thighs are parallel to the ground. Roll your shoulders down and back and press your tailbone toward the ground.

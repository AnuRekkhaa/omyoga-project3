Yoga for Lifelong Vitality

1.Improved Flexibility:Regular yoga practice enhances flexibility, helping to maintain mobility and prevent injury.
2.Better Balance:Practicing balance poses reduces the risk of falls and promotes overall stability.
3.Pain Management:Gentle stretching and movement can alleviate chronic pain and stiffness.

Tips:
1.Start Slow:Begin with gentle classes tailored for seniors to build confidence and avoid injury.
2.Listen to Your Body:Pay attention to your body’s signals and avoid pushing yourself into discomfort or pain.
3.Modify Poses:Don’t hesitate to modify poses or use alternatives that suit your comfort level and ability.

IMPORTANT AASANAS

Side Warrior Pose

Pivot your right foot 90 deg to the right while angling your left foot slightly inward.Place your hands on your waist, grounding through the feet and lengthening through the crown, or top of the head.Breathe deeply, and on your exhale, bend your right knee with the ankle below it.

Reclining Twist

Press your feet into the floor, lift your hips and shift them to the left.Shift your weight onto your right hip.Extend your arms out from your shoulders to about shoulder height, palms facing up.Rest your right hand on your left thigh to ease the knees down toward the floor.

Tree Pose

Stand tall like a tree, rooting one foot firmly into the ground while the other leg is placed against the inner thigh or calf of the standing leg. Enhances balance, strengthens the legs, and opens the hips while encouraging mindfulness and focus.

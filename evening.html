EVENING SERENITY

Evening yoga is a restorative practice designed to help you unwind after a busy day. This calming routine promotes relaxation, releases tension, and prepares the mind and body for a restful night.

BENEFITS
1.Promotes Better Sleep: Practicing yoga in the evening can lead to improved sleep quality by calming the nervous system.
2.Enhances Flexibility: Gentle stretches in the morning promote flexibility and reduce stiffness after a night's rest.
3.Supports Recovery: Ideal for soothing tired muscles and aiding recovery from daily activities or workouts.
4.Reduces Stress: Establishing a morning routine with yoga can reduce anxiety and promote a sense of calm.

Your Evening Yoga Routine

1.Gentle Warm-ups: Start with soft stretches to release tension.
2.Restorative Poses: Incorporate poses like Child’s Pose or Legs-Up-the-Wall to promote relaxation.
3.Twists and Hips Openers: Include gentle twists and hip openers to release any built-up tension.
4.Closing Poses: End with calming postures or meditation to center yourself.

Tips:
1.Create a Calm Atmosphere: Set up a peaceful space with soft lighting or candles.
2.Focus on Your Breath: Emphasize deep, mindful breathing to enhance relaxation.
3.Listen to Your Body: Move gently and adapt poses to your comfort level.

IMPORTANT AASANAS

Standing Forward Fold

Stand with your feet wide apart and parallel to each other. Inhale and raise your arms to shoulder height, then exhale and fold forward from the hips.Hold your back straight and rest your hands on the floor, blocks, or your legs.

Seated Spinal Twist

Sit on the floor with your legs stretched in front of you. Bend your right knee and position your right foot on the outside of your left knee. Bring your left elbow to the outside of your right knee and twist your torso to the right.

Sphinx Pose

By lying on the stomach with the elbows beneath the shoulders and the forearms on the ground, lifting the head and chest off the mat while keeping the pubic bone grounded.
